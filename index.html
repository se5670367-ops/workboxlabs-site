WorkBox AI · Local AI Worker

A workstation-based AI worker for coding, analysis, documentation, and real business operations tasks — all on hardware you own.

WorkBox AI behaves more like a focused software engineer and operations assistant than a chatbot. It streams its desktop, takes on structured tasks, and works end-to-end on code, documents, prototypes or planning workflows — with every input and file optically ingressed, sanitized, and under your control.

What makes WorkBox different?

Most AI tools live in the cloud and ask you to upload everything. WorkBox AI flips that model: it lives on your machine, looks at a local desktop session, and works inside an environment you own.

  • Local models, local files, local compute.
  • Tasks expressed in plain language and JSON, not scripts you have to write.
  • Optional pairing with the GlassBox Isolator for higher-risk experiments.
Overview

What is WorkBox AI for?

WorkBox AI is a local AI worker that helps with the kind of work people do at a desk: writing and refactoring code, preparing documentation, exploring ideas, and supporting business operations tasks like reports, small tools and structured analysis.

Not just a chat window

WorkBox is designed around tasks, not endless conversation. You point it at a project or problem, frame the task, and let it work inside a controlled desktop session.

The goal isn’t to replace all work — it’s to offload the heavy lifting on the parts that are repetitive, mechanical or exploratory, while keeping you firmly in charge of direction and approval.

Lives where your work already is

Because WorkBox runs on a workstation, it is naturally close to your code, documents and tools. It doesn’t need to send your files to an external API to be useful.

That makes it especially suited for internal projects, prototypes, experiments, and environments where cloud AI is either not allowed or not trusted.

Capabilities

What a WorkBox can help with

The first versions of WorkBox AI focus on a handful of useful, concrete roles. Over time, these can be combined into richer workflows, but each starts from a simple, understandable capability.

Role
Software assistant

Take a repository, describe the goal, and let the WorkBox propose changes, refactors, scaffolding or tests. You stay in control of commits and deployment.

  • Refactor or simplify existing code
  • Add tests and documentation
  • Sketch small tools or utilities
Role
Documentation & analysis worker

Turn rough notes, specs or transcripts into structured, readable material you can share with a team or client.

  • Summaries and short briefs
  • How-to guides and checklists
  • Change logs and release notes
Role
Operations helper

Support repetitive operations work like data clean-up, simple reports, schedule proposals or small admin automations, always with you in the loop.

  • Structured spreadsheets & CSV clean-up
  • Drafting simple internal tools
  • Generating options, not final decisions
Safety model

File ingestion and safety are not an afterthought

Uploading files to a WorkBox is not the same as uploading them to a cloud AI. There is no “just drag everything into the browser and hope for the best”. All inputs pass through a controlled, optically-bounded pipeline.

Optical-only, curated ingress

In the full WorkBox + Isolator setup, files and prompts are transferred via structured optical bursts — the same philosophy as the GlassBox Isolator.

That means:

  • No direct network uploads into a remote model.
  • Content can be filtered, redacted or abstracted before the AI ever sees it.
  • You can decide which parts of a file are relevant — not the model.
Sanitized context, not raw personal data

The aim is for WorkBox to work on sanitized, structured context, not raw piles of sensitive information. Over time, this can include:

  • Whitelists of allowed patterns and fields
  • Curator passes that strip identifiers and metadata
  • Clear separation between private archives and working prompts

The result: a worker that can meaningfully help, without needing to see everything.

Workflow

How a WorkBox task runs

A WorkBox session is meant to feel like working alongside a very fast, very patient colleague who happens to live inside a dedicated workstation.

Step 1
Frame the task

You describe the goal in plain language: what project, what constraints, what “good” looks like, and any inputs you want the WorkBox to use.

Step 2
The worker explores

The AI runs inside the WorkBox desktop, generating code, drafts, or analyses. You can watch the screen or review outputs after the fact.

Step 3
You review the outputs

You inspect changes, drafts or proposals. Nothing is forced into production: it’s all material for you to accept, adapt or discard.

Step 4
Approve, refine, repeat

Once you’re happy, you apply changes, copy results into your own tools, or queue another task. The loop is designed to be incremental and reversible.
